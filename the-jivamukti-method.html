What is Jivamukti Yoga?

Jivamukti Yoga is a path to enlightenment through compassion for all beings. The word Jivamukti is made of two Sanskrit words — jiva meaning 'individual living soul' and mukti meaning 'liberation.' Yoga means connection — our relationship to the Earth and all beings.

Using asana as a method to attain Self-realization means exploring the true meaning of the term — our relationship to the Earth, to other humans, animals, plants, and all of manifestation. All Jivamukti Yoga classes integrate five central tenets that make the practice transformative beyond the physical.

The Five Tenets

Present in every Jivamukti class, these five practices form the foundation of the method.

Ahimsa

अहिंसा

Non-violence. A compassionate lifestyle extending to other animals, the environment, and all living beings, emphasizing ethical veganism and animal rights.

Bhakti

भक्ति

Devotion. Acknowledgment that God/Self-realization is the goal of all yoga practices. Humility, the recognition of a higher power.

Dhyana

ध्यान

Meditation. Connecting to that unchanging reality within — the eternal, absolute consciousness.

Nada

नाद

Deep listening. The development of a sound body and mind through deep listening, including chanting, kirtan, and music.

Shastra

शास्त्र

Scripture. Study of the ancient yogic teachings, including Sanskrit chanting, to access the depth of the living wisdom.

Activism & Innovations

Jivamukti Yoga has always been at the intersection of spiritual practice and practical activism. From advocating for animal rights and ethical veganism to environmental protection, the method encourages practitioners to take their practice off the mat and into the world.
